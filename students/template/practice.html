Medium Mechanics
Question 1

A ball is thrown vertically upward with an initial velocity of 20 m/s. Neglecting air resistance, what is the maximum height reached by the ball? (Take g = 10 m/s²)

Use the equation v² = u² + 2as, where v is the final velocity (which is zero at maximum height).

At maximum height, the velocity becomes zero.

Using v² = u² + 2as

0 = (20)² + 2(-10)s

s = 20 meters

Medium Thermodynamics
Question 2

A heat engine operates between temperatures of 500 K and 300 K. What is the maximum possible efficiency of this engine?

Use the Carnot efficiency formula: η = 1 - T₂/T₁, where T₁ is the hot reservoir temperature and T₂ is the cold reservoir temperature.

Maximum efficiency (Carnot efficiency) = 1 - T₂/T₁

η = 1 - 300/500

η = 1 - 0.6

η = 0.4 or 40%

Medium Electromagnetism
Question 3

A wire of length 2 meters carries a current of 5 amperes. If the wire is placed in a uniform magnetic field of 0.5 tesla at an angle of 30° to the field, what is the magnitude of the force experienced by the wire?

Use the formula F = ILB sin θ, where I is the current, L is the length, B is the magnetic field, and θ is the angle between the wire and the field.

F = ILB sin θ

F = 5 × 2 × 0.5 × sin(30°)

F = 5 × 2 × 0.5 × 0.5

F = 2.5 newtons
